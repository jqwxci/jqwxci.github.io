Jane Eyre

by Charlotte Brontë

About the Book

"Jane Eyre" is a novel by Charlotte Brontë, published in 1847. It is a classic work of literature that tells the story of Jane Eyre, an orphan who becomes a governess and falls in love with her employer, Mr. Rochester.

Plot Summary

The novel explores themes of love, morality, and social class. It follows Jane's journey from a harsh childhood to her independence as a strong and principled woman.

Why I Love It

"Jane Eyre" is my favorite book because of its compelling characters, the strength of Jane's character, and its timeless themes that still resonate today.
